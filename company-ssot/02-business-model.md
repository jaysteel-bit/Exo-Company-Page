# Business Model

## What Exo Sells

Exo Enterprise sells a **service-software hybrid** called the X-Scale Department Program. This is not pure consulting and not pure SaaS — it is both.

The pitch in plain terms: We design the AI department strategy, install our proprietary operating system (Flow OS) to run it, co-operate the department alongside the client's team for 90 days, then transfer complete ownership to the client. The client walks away owning a fully operational internal department.

## The Delivery Model: Build → Operate → Transfer (BOM-T)

Exo uses a structured three-phase methodology:
1. **Build** — Architects diagnose operational chaos, map workflows, and blueprint the department OS
2. **Operate** — Engineers install Flow OS, deploy AI agents, and automate the department
3. **Transfer** — Conductors co-run the department, certify the client's team through Exo Academy, write all SOPs, and deliver the "Sovereignty Packet" (all IP, data, documentation)

The end deliverable is:
- A configured instance of Flow OS running the client's operations
- A trained internal team certified in the system
- A Sovereignty Packet: all SOPs, IP, and operational data
- Zero vendor lock-in — the client owns 100% of the output

## Offer Structure

**Primary offer: X-Scale Department Program**
- Flagship product, high-touch engagement
- 90-day transformation timeline
- Highly selective: fewer than 10% of applicants are accepted
- Limited to 8 partners per month (scarcity is real — not just a marketing device)

**Lead qualifier on the application form:**
- Annual revenue: ideal sweet spot is $2M–$50M
- Must be able to invest in the business
- Entry price communicated on the form as "We start at $2k" (this is the qualifying floor; full program investment is different)

## Pricing

Exo does not publish a fixed price list. Custom departments require custom scoping. However, based on what is stated publicly on the sales page:

- Entry acknowledgment: "We start at $2k" (qualifying question, not program price)
- Full program investment range: **$15,000–$60,000** for a complete transformation
- This investment range is positioned against the cost of bad hires and broken tools — the framing: "If you haven't wasted at least that much in the last year, you probably don't need us yet."

## Revenue Model

- Project-based engagements (primary current model)
- Future arc: BOM-T model implies a potential SaaS / platform layer once product-market fit is established (Flow OS as a recurring software subscription post-transfer)
- Steel is a separate B2C/membership business model (see 06-product.md)

## Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

- Founders and operators of companies generating **$2M–$50M in annual revenue**
- Experiencing the "Empty Middle" problem: growing fast enough to break processes, but not large enough to have enterprise infrastructure
- The founder is the bottleneck — every decision runs through one person
- Has already tried tools or consultants that didn't deliver
- Ready and able to invest $15k–$60k to solve an operational problem that is costing more than that

**Disqualifying profile:**
- Under $2M ARR → Likely needs lead generation or PMF work, not ops scale-up
- Over $100M → Dedicated Enterprise division handles this (separate track)
