# Product

## Tech-Stack / Product Architecture Overview

Exo operates a **modular department ecosystem** under a single operating platform. Think of it as a holding company structure for AI departments:

- **Flow OS** — the operating system / platform layer; all departments run on top of it
- **Department Namespaces** — modular AI-powered departments clients can install
- **Steel** — separate B2C consumer brand running on the same underlying platform

---

## Flow OS — The Core Platform

**What it is:** Flow OS is Exo's proprietary operating system for autonomous departments. It is the software layer that gets installed, configured, and then transferred to the client.

**What it does:**
- Centralizes operational data across the company
- Automates workflows and deploys AI "reflex agents" (autonomous decision-making units)
- Maintains institutional memory that compounds over time
- Runs departments continuously without constant human oversight

**How it's described publicly:**
> "We install our proprietary operating system. Centralize your data, automate diffs, and deploy reflex agents."

**Positioning:**
Flow OS is positioned as a "Unified AI Operations Layer" — the badge used on flow.html. It is not positioned as software that the client uses independently; it is positioned as a backbone that Exo installs, operates, and then trains the client's team to own.

**Current stage:** Early product, described in SEO.md as "very early stage with unproven ICP and no fully built product." The approach is high-touch service-first, with software maturing as client proof points accumulate.

**Positioning vs. alternatives:**
- vs. Pure consulting: "Consulting leaves you with a strategy but no execution"
- vs. Pure software: "Software leaves you with a tool but no process"
- Exo: "A hybrid firm — we consult, install, then transfer"

Each namespace is a specific functional department that can be installed via Flow OS:

## Steel — The B2C Consumer Brand

**What it is:** Steel by Exo is an invitation-only identity and membership ecosystem — "access redefined." Think: a premium NFC identity card that lets members tap-to-share their profile, access exclusive venues, and control exactly who gets their contact information.

**How it works:**
- Members carry a Steel card (physical NFC)
- Tap the card → verification state → profile unlock → share contacts, social links, connect
- The profile demo on steel.html shows: name, location, Instagram, LinkedIn, phone, "Add to Contacts"

**Value props:** Seamless access to exclusive venues/experiences, controlled connection sharing, unified identity for payments + networking + membership

**Status:** Waitlist phase. Invitations Q3 2026. Target: First 10,000 members.
**Positioning:** "The definitive identity ecosystem for the exceptional. Invitation-only."

Steel is explicitly positioned as a **separate company feel** but powered by Exo/Flow OS infrastructure. Future standalone domain: steelbyexo.com (currently steelglobal.us registered).

## Internal Role Structure

Exo's delivery team operates across three function types:

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Architects** | Diagnose operational chaos, map workflows, blueprint the department OS |
| **Engineers** | Install Flow OS, deploy AI agents, build automations |
| **Conductors** | Co-run departments with clients, certify through Exo Academy, deliver Sovereignty Packet |

All three roles operate in overlap on every engagement. Collectively, all Exo personnel carry the designation **Extraordinaire**.
