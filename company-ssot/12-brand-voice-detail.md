# Brand Voice & Copywriting Guidelines

---

## The Core Rule

**Give them the vacation, not the airport process.**

We never describe the mechanism (BOM/T phases, diffs, reflex agents) without leading with the outcome. The prospect feels what life looks like after — the Tuesday morning where they open their laptop and everything is already running.

---

## Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Intelligent** | We don't dumb it down, but we don't show off. Precision over jargon. |
| **Confident** | We make claims and back them with specifics. No hedging. |
| **Direct** | Short sentences. No filler. Say the thing. |
| **Human** | We write like a smart operator talking to another smart operator. No corporate speak. |
| **Outcome-first** | Lead with what they get, not what we do. |

---

## Tone By Context

**Homepage / hero:** Bold, outcome-focused, creates urgency without hype. The reader should feel like we already understand their problem before they finish the first sentence.

**Sales page (x-scale.html):** Cinematic, narrative-driven. Tell the story of their current chaos, then introduce the system as the relief. Testimonials anchor the promise.

**Product pages (flow.html):** More technical but still outcome-led. The features serve the outcomes — never lead with features.

**Firm / About page:** Authoritative and human. We are founders and operators, not consultants.

## Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the outcome, not the method.**  
   Bad: "Our BOM/T model delivers phased AI transformation."  
   Good: "We build your department, run it until it's flawless, then hand you the keys."

2. **Use specifics over generalities.**  
   Bad: "We help you save time."  
   Good: "Most founders spend 15 hours a week on coordination they'll never get back."

3. **Name the pain before you offer the solution.**  
   The prospect needs to feel seen before they'll believe the fix.

4. **Avoid jargon when writing for prospects.**  
   - BOM/T → "Build it, run it, hand it to you"
   - Diffs architecture → "Every decision your business makes gets logged and learned from"
   - Reflex agents → "The system acts before you even notice the problem"

5. **Short sentences in hero copy. Slightly longer in explanatory sections.**  
   The rhythm of short + long creates emphasis. Don't make every sentence the same length.

6. **Use "you" more than "we."**  
   The reader should be the hero of the story. We're the guide.

7. **Contrast before/after states.**  
   Paint the messy before clearly so the after lands harder.

8. **Quantify whenever possible.**  
   "25 hours back in month one" beats "significant time savings."

## The Tuesday Morning Frame

Our most powerful narrative device. Used in the VSL and across sales copy:

> "Tuesday morning. You open your laptop. You're not the first one in. The system already ran the standup. Deals moved without you touching them. Three proposals went out overnight. Your pipeline updated itself. And your team? They already know what to do."

This is the dream state we're selling. Return to this image whenever we need to land the emotional close.

---

## The F1 Pit Crew Analogy

Used to explain our operating model:

> "A Formula 1 driver doesn't pull over mid-race and try to change his own tires. A pit crew of specialists runs out, executes 20 precise jobs in under three seconds, and sends him back out faster than he came in. That's us. You stay focused on driving the business. We handle the rebuild."

---

## The "Door #3" Framework

Used to position us against the market's two bad options:

- Door #1: Hire consultants who might underdeliver
- Door #2: Get great results but become permanently dependent
- Door #3 (Exo): We build it, you own it

## What We Don't Say

- "AI-powered" as the leading feature (everyone says this now)
- "Digital transformation"
- "Synergy"
- "Robust" or "comprehensive"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Leverage" as a standalone promise (say what gets leveraged)
- "Disruptive" or "innovative"
- "We're not just another [X]" — show, don't disclaim

---

## Exo AI Character Voice (VSL Narrator)

When writing from the Exo AI perspective (VSL, narration copy):

- Speaks in first person as "Exo"
- Not aggressive or hype-y — calm authority, like a brilliant operator who has seen your exact problem before and already knows the solution
- Meta-awareness: "I'm the AI narrating the VSL I'm also selling" — lean into this
- Opens with: "My name is Exo. I'm the AI Commander of Exo Enterprise."
- Tempo: measured, intentional. Leaves pauses in the script for TTS pacing.
