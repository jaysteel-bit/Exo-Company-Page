# Sales

## The Sales Process (Lead to Closed Deal)

Exo uses an application-gated model, not an open-enrollment or direct-pitch model. The process is:

1. **Awareness** — Paid ads (Meta/Facebook confirmed via pixel), organic content, lead magnets
2. **Lead magnet opt-in** — Prospect visits value.html (Exo Vault), submits name/email/company, gets free resources (scaling roadmap, workflow maps, SOP templates)
3. **Lead flows to application page** — Session data transfers form fields from value.html to x-scale.html; prospect is pre-qualified before the form
4. **Application form** (x-scale.html):
   - Name, work email, phone
   - Qualifying questions revealed after phone validation:
     - Annual revenue (Under $100k → $30M+)
     - "Are you able to invest in your business?" (Yes / No / Maybe)
     - "We start at $2k. Does that work for you?" (Yes / No)
5. **Calendar booking** — Qualified applicants click through to book a 15-minute Discovery Call ("Zero pressure, consultation only")
6. **Discovery call** — Admission team assesses fit (not a traditional sales pitch)
7. **Acceptance** — Fewer than 10% of applicants are accepted; this exclusivity is communicated openly as a brand signal
8. **Onboarding** — Client enters the BOM-T transformation engagement

## Sales Framing

The primary sales frame is **removal of risk and removal of the founder bottleneck**, not features or deliverables. The X-Scale page leads with:

> "ARE YOU THE BIGGEST RISK TO YOUR OWN BUSINESS?"

The framing is: the founder being indispensable is not a badge of honor — it is a structural liability. Exo removes that liability.

The sub-framing: "Letting you work ON the business, not kept STUCK IN it."

## Objection Handling

The FAQ section on x-scale.html is the primary objection-handling vehicle. Key objections and responses:

**Objection 1: "Is this a consultancy or a software product?"**
*Answer:* "It is both. High-end consulting leaves you with a strategy but no execution. Software leaves you with a tool but no process. We are a Hybrid Firm. We consult to design the strategy, then install our software (Flow OS) to execute it. Most importantly, we transfer the entire capability to you within 90 days."

**Objection 2: "What exactly do I walk away with?"**
*Answer:* "A fully operational internal department. Specifically: 1) A configured instance of Flow OS running your ops, 2) A trained team (yours or new hires) certified in the system, and 3) A 'Sovereignty Packet' containing all SOPs, IP, and data. You own 100% of the output."

**Objection 3: "Is this right for my business size?"**
*Answer:* "Our ideal partners are generating between $2M and $50M in annual revenue. If you are smaller, you likely need a lead generation or PMF solution, not an operations scale-up. If you are larger ($100M+), we have a dedicated Enterprise division. This program is optimized for the 'Empty Middle' where growth breaks processes."

**Objection 4: "How much does the transformation cost?"**
*Answer:* "We don't publish a price list because we build custom departments. However, partners typically invest between $15k and $60k for a complete transformation. If you haven't wasted at least that much on bad hires or broken tools in the last year, you probably don't need us yet."

**Objection 5: "What if I need ongoing support after transfer?"**
*Implied answer:* "If you miss us, we're always a click away." Zero vendor lock-in is a feature — the relationship doesn't have to end, but the dependency does.

## What Gets Someone Over the Line

The primary closer is the **sovereignty framing**: the client doesn't just get better operations — they get to *own* those operations forever. The "Sovereignty Packet" as a branded deliverable (SOPs, IP, data) makes the handover feel like receiving an asset, not ending a service.

Secondary closers:
- Scarcity: 8 spots/month, real capacity constraint
- Social proof: testimonials tied to specific, measurable outcomes (close rate 15% → 42% in 6 weeks; scaled ops without hiring a single new manager)
- Application gatekeeping as proof of quality: if fewer than 10% get in, the clients who do are validated as real operators

## What "No" Usually Means

- **Wrong price/timing** — Most common. Business revenue is real but the owner isn't ready to invest or is mid-crisis. The "$2k qualifier" on the form filters this before calls.
- **Wrong size** — Business is pre-PMF or pre-$2M; doesn't need ops scale-up yet. Acknowledged openly — Exo doesn't try to sell down-market.
- **Wrong problem** — Some prospects have an operations problem that isn't yet bad enough to justify a $15k–$60k investment. They typically return after more pain.

## Guarantee

**Current guarantee:** 55% efficiency gain — or we don't stop working.

This is a commitment to results, not a money-back clause. If the client doesn't hit 55% efficiency improvement, Exo continues working until they do. There is no refund option and no "work for free" framing — the language is "we don't stop" which keeps the relationship as a partnership, not a liability.

**Scrapped:** "20% speed lift in 30 days or money back" — removed. Do not use.
**Scrapped:** Any "work for free" language — removed. Do not use.

The guarantee communicates confidence in the outcome, not desperation to close.
