## Brand Philosophy: Veblen Goods Strategy

Steel's pricing and positioning should follow a Veblen goods model — aspirational flagship items that are extremely hard to acquire drive demand and price-tolerance for the broader accessible product line.

**The mechanism:**
- High-end "Veblen" items (limited, exclusive, premium-priced) maintain brand prestige
- Entry-level items benefit from the prestige halo — consumers pay full price because the brand is aspirational
- Managing dilution: avoid discount cycles, maintain "artificial scarcity," control distribution

**Key principles from research:**
- Price entry-level items relative to the most expensive pieces (anchoring)
- Use round numbers only ($100, not $99.99) — signals intentionality, not discount
- Unboxing/service experience for a $100 item should match the $10,000 item
- Never put entry-level products in discount cycles or mass-market retailers

**Sub-branding note:** If we need a significantly lower price point, a sub-brand creates distance (e.g., Miu Miu by Prada). In Steel's case: "Global by Steel" was floated but may be confusing given "Steel by Exo" + "Steel Global" already coexist. Needs a naming decision before committing.

## Product Tier System (Draft)

Three-tier internal architecture:

| Tier | Label | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Entry | Steel (Standard) | Accessible, high-quality products for the general public |
| Mid | Steel+ (Premium) | Enhanced features, higher quality materials |
| Top | Steel++ (Luxury/Veblen) | Extremely limited, premium materials, exclusive designs — flagship tier |

The Steel++ tier is the prestige anchor. It does not need to drive volume; it drives brand value that makes Steel and Steel+ feel aspirational.

## Ecommerce Product Portfolio (Brainstorm)

**Direction:** NFC-infused products that link to owner's profile/digital presence OR verify authenticity/ownership. Fighting counterfeit goods angle. Product exclusivity via NFC.

**Categories to explore:**

*Non-common / Veblen items:*
- High-end pool table with Steel branding + unique design
- Premium chairs with Steel design
- Men's/Women's jewelry box / briefcase with biometric fingerprint lock + Steel logo
- One-of-a-kind or Virgil-collaboration concept items

*Accessible luxury line:*
- Hoodies: $200 entry → $500+ premium range
- Other lifestyle apparel

**Open question:** Is the brand more "quality over uniqueness" or quality + uniqueness? The current leaning is quality-first, which would push toward fewer, better items rather than a wide catalog.

**Unsettled:** Whether to pursue unique/collaboration items. May dilute the identity-focused brand if not done carefully.

## Revenue Model (Draft)

Not finalized. Current thinking:

- **Physical products:** Direct-to-consumer via Steel website. High margins on limited items. Volume on entry tier.
- **Digital (Steel App / steel.id):** Freemium or subscription. Monetize premium profile features.
- **Steel Global membership:** Tiered citizenship. Likely subscription or one-time initiation.
- **B2B (Steel for Teams):** Enterprise licensing for branded identity infrastructure.
- **Brand partnerships / world sponsorships:** Brands pay to sponsor editorial worlds inside Steel Global.

Downsell strategy: needs dedicated development. → See open task below.
