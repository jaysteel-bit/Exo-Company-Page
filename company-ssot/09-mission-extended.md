## The BOM/T Delivery Model

BOM/T = **Build → Operate → Manage/Transfer**

This is our core service architecture. It is a key differentiator — but we never lead with the acronym. We lead with the outcome (the vacation, not the airport process).

### Phase 1 — Build (Weeks 1–4 / Month 1)
- Senior Architects audit your entire operation — workflows, data sources, decision points.
- We identify the real structural bottleneck (not the surface symptom).
- Quick wins first: automated handoffs, unified data layer, bleeding stopped.
- Client commitment: ~2 hours/week. We do the rest.
- Deliverable: Complete operational blueprint + early Flow OS build.

### Phase 2 — Operate (Weeks 5–12 / Months 2–3)
- We co-run the department alongside your team.
- Every decision gets logged as structured data (our "diffs" architecture).
- Training happens through doing — your team learns the system by operating it.
- Flow OS is fully configured to your workflows.

### Phase 3 — Manage/Transfer (Month 3 onwards)
- Structured handover: trained team, documented SOPs, full Sovereignty Packet.
- You walk away with a self-running department you own outright.
- Optional ongoing advisory retainer.
- Guarantee: If we don't hit 55% efficiency gains, we keep working until we do.

## Three Engagement Paths

Clients choose based on their team readiness:

1. **Rebuild Path** — We implement Flow OS + upskill existing team through Exo Academy. You take ownership when ready.
2. **Recruit Path** — We stabilize with Flow OS, then use AURA to source and onboard a new high-performing team.
3. **Managed Path** — We run the department for you indefinitely; transfer happens on your timeline.
