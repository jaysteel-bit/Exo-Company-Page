  Question 1 — Selling Departments Individually vs. Bundled

  The honest answer is: both, but in a specific structure.

  The reason the bundle is more valuable right now is that Flow OS is the integration layer. Deal OS running inside Flow OS, connected   to Exo Academy and Exo Launch, sharing the same data model — that's the product. Deal OS as a standalone tool is just another sales   intelligence SaaS competing against Gong ($7.5B), Clari ($1.6B), and 200 others. That's a brutal market to enter from scratch.     

  The smarter architecture:

  Flow OS = The Platform (the Apple iOS)
    ↓
  Departments = Apps on the platform (can be purchased individually OR bundled)
    ↓
  X-Scale = The "iPhone purchase" — you get the device + apps + setup all at once

  So the revenue model becomes:

  | Purchase Path | Who It's For | Price |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| X-Scale Full Program | Companies who want everything installed | $40-60K+ |
| Flow OS Foundation + à la carte namespaces | Companies already on Flow OS, adding departments | $300-2K/month per namespace |
| Namespace license only (no consulting) | Self-serve, post-PMF | $299-999/month |     

  The carve-out for EV purposes:
  If Deal OS reaches $2-3M standalone ARR, you raise a Series A for just that namespace — sell 20-25% of Deal OS equity to a VC at a  
  6-8x multiple, take $10-15M off the table, and keep 75%+ of Deal OS plus 100% of the holdco. The holdco retains the namespace, you  
  get capital, and Deal OS becomes a funded product that can compete in its own market. You never sold the company. You sold a slice  
  of one department.

  That's the long-term play. Right now the priority is building the system that makes these departments worth something — not selling 
  them before they exist.

| Plan | Annual (upfront) | Monthly |
|------|-----------------|---------|
