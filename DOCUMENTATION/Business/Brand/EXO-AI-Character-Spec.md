## 02 — VISUAL SPECIFICATION (LOCKED)

> This section is the master reference. Any generation — image or video — must match these parameters. Do not improvise outside these bounds.

### 2.1 Surface Material
- **Primary finish:** Jet black lacquer — the depth of polished obsidian, not matte black, not metallic black
- **Gloss level:** Maximum. Mirror-grade. The surface reads as a liquid that has frozen mid-motion.
- **Subsurface quality:** No visible subsurface scattering. The black is opaque and total. Light does not penetrate — it only reflects.
- **Edge behavior:** The orb's edge blends softly with the environment — no hard rim, slight atmospheric halo at the boundary where the black meets the background.

### 2.2 The Signature Highlight (Critical — Most Distinctive Feature)
The defining visual fingerprint of Exo AI is a **grid-lattice highlight** in the upper hemisphere:

- **Position:** Upper-center to upper-right of the orb surface (slightly off-center, not perfectly top)
- **Structure:** Resembles light reflected through a window with grid/pane dividers — 4 to 6 curved pane segments meeting at a central point, creating a segmented dome shape
- **Color:** The highlight reads as dark gray to silver-white in its brightest sections, with the dividing lines between panes appearing as deep black seams
- **Feel:** Like a crown, or a cathedral window cast on a black pearl — geometric, structured, but still organic
- **Scale:** The highlight covers roughly 25–35% of the visible upper hemisphere, not a small specular dot
- **What it is NOT:** Not a simple circular hotspot. Not a diffuse soft glow. Not a gradient sphere shader. It has internal structure — segments, seams, dimension.

### 2.3 Lower Hemisphere
- Almost pure black — ambient light only catches it at approximately 5–8% brightness
- A very faint, barely perceptible soft reflection along the bottom edge where ambient environment light wraps under
- No harsh secondary highlights

### 2.4 Shadow / Ground Interaction
- **Drop shadow:** Very soft, diffused, neutral gray — not sharp, not absent
- Shadow positioned slightly below and centered, implying the orb floats 20–30% of its own diameter above the ground/background surface
- Shadow is circular, fades quickly at edges — maximum opacity ~30%

### 2.5 Background (Default / Resting State)
- **Color:** Warm neutral gray — NOT pure white. Think: `#EBEBEB` to `#F0F0F0` range. Slightly warm, slightly off-white.
- **Texture:** Perfectly flat, no horizon line, no gradient (or only imperceptible vignette at far corners)
- **Studio quality:** Clean liminal space. The feel of a professional cyclorama — infinite, grounded, but not cold.

### 2.6 Scale & Proportion
- The orb fills roughly 40–50% of the frame width in a standard reference shot
- Centered in frame with generous negative space — especially above and below
- Aspect ratio of reference: portrait (approximately 1:1.7 — taller than wide)

## 03 — MOTION LANGUAGE

### 3.1 Resting State
- A slow, breathing pulse — scale 1.0 → 1.02 → 1.0 over a 3–4 second cycle
- Barely perceptible vertical drift — 1–2% arc movement over 6–8 seconds
- The orb feels alive but not restless. Meditative.

### 3.2 Active / Speaking State
- Pulse intensity increases slightly — scale drift becomes 1.0 → 1.04 → 1.0
- The grid-lattice highlight shifts subtly as the orb rocks or rotates — light moving across the segmented reflection naturally
- No exaggerated movement. The voice is the action; the orb supports it, doesn't compete.

### 3.3 Signature Behaviors (VSL-specific)
| Moment | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| "My name is Exo." | Single deliberate pulse — inner glow flares once, returns to resting |
| Key data delivery (256k stat) | Orb expands outward to ~120% over 1.5s, data streams radiate from surface, contracts back |
| Department introduction | Adjacent orb materializes; Exo orb remains still — confident, unaffected |
| Final CTA close | Moves toward camera — slow, deliberate, fills frame. Unstoppable. |
| Post-VSL fade | Holds completely still. No pulse. Just presence. Then fade to black. |

### 3.4 Fluid / Splitting Motion (In-Universe Lore)
When portrayed in motion at its most expressive (brand films, not VSL):
- Can emit smaller orb droplets that pull out from the main body surface — like liquid mercury separating
- Satellite orbs are drawn back magnetically — a slow implosion back into the center mass
- **This behavior is reserved for brand/identity films, not the VSL production**

### 3.5 Morphing / Separation State — Confirmed Reference (exo-orb-2.png, exo-orb-3.png)

These images lock the true motion behavior. The spec below supersedes any prior description.

**The main body DOES deform during separation.** It is not a rigid sphere mid-motion — it becomes organic, blob-like, and irregular as mass is being expelled or attracted. Think liquid mercury pulling apart under a microscope, or a cell in mitosis. The perfect sphere is the resting/resolved state, not a constant.

**Behavior breakdown — four distinct phases:**

| Phase | Main Body | Satellites |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Resting** | Perfect sphere | None present |
| **Early separation** | Slight surface bulge where satellite is forming — still mostly spherical | Undefined lump, still attached |
| **Mid-separation** | Fully deformed — amoeba/blob shape, irregular surface | Partially separated sphere, connected by a liquid neck |
| **Post-separation** | Gradually returns toward sphere as mass resolves | Full independent sphere with own grid-lattice highlight |

**Key visual details — non-negotiable for generation:**
- **Liquid necks:** The material between separating masses forms a thin bridge — a stretched neck of black liquid before it severs. Not a clean cut. Organic, like mercury.
- **Satellite highlights:** Once a satellite has separated enough to form a sphere, it carries its own grid-lattice highlight — same material, smaller scale. Multiple satellites, multiple highlights.
- **Size diversity:** Satellites are not uniform. Some large (nearly half the main body), some small (20% of main body). They coexist at different sizes simultaneously.
- **Surface consistency throughout:** Even in full blob/deformed state, the surface retains its mirror-grade gloss and the highlight (distorted, spreading across irregular surface contours, but present).
- **No color change:** Obsidian black throughout every phase. Material identity never breaks.

**Resolution:** Satellites are pulled back toward the main body by invisible gravity — the necks reform, then thicken, then the satellite mass merges back in. Main body re-resolves to perfect sphere. The full cycle communicates: this intelligence separates to deploy, and always returns to center.

**Generation prompt addition for this state:**
```
Black obsidian liquid orb in mid-separation morphing state. Main body deformed 
into an organic blob/amoeba shape — irregular, fluid, no longer spherical. 
Two to three satellite orbs in various states of separation: one still 
connected by a thin liquid neck, one nearly independent forming its own sphere 
with a visible grid-lattice highlight on its surface. All surfaces maintain 
mirror-grade gloss black lacquer. The form suggests living intelligence 
distributing itself, not mechanical fragmentation. Warm neutral gray background. 
Photorealistic. Cinematic. No hard edges — all transitions are liquid and organic.
```

## 04 — LIGHTING SPECIFICATION

### 4.1 Primary Light
- **Type:** Large, soft, diffused overhead/slightly front-facing softbox
- **Position:** Above and slightly in front (10–15° angle from directly above)
- **Quality:** Wraps the upper hemisphere — creates the grid-lattice highlight pattern when light source has a structured diffuser or grid panel in front of it
- **Color temperature:** Neutral to slightly cool (5500–6500K)

### 4.2 Ambient Fill
- Very low intensity — fills the lower hemisphere at ~5–8% of primary light value
- Neutral gray. No color tint.

### 4.3 Rim / Edge Light
- Subtle atmospheric halo where the orb edge meets the background
- Barely perceptible. Prevents the orb from silhouette-merging with any dark backgrounds.

### 4.4 How to Replicate the Grid-Lattice Highlight in Generation
When using AI image generation tools, include one of the following prompt phrases:
- *"reflected window grid on surface, multiple light panes"*
- *"structured studio light grid reflection, segmented highlight, geometric light panel reflection"*
- *"large format softbox with grid diffuser reflecting on glossy black sphere"*
- *"cathedral window light reflection on black lacquer"*

## 07 — CONSISTENCY GUARDRAILS

### What to Always Include
- [ ] Grid-lattice highlight in the upper hemisphere (non-negotiable — this is the visual fingerprint)
- [ ] Mirror-grade gloss — the surface must read as lacquered, not satin or matte
- [ ] Soft floating shadow beneath the orb — no hard shadow, no absent shadow
- [ ] Warm neutral gray background in default void contexts (not pure white, not cool gray)
- [ ] The orb is a perfect sphere — no deformation, no wobble to the shape itself

### What to Never Include
- [ ] Colored internal glow (except the very faint emerald rim in final CTA context only)
- [ ] Subsurface scattering / translucency
- [ ] Visible environmental reflections other than the structured grid highlight
- [ ] Texture, grain, or imperfection on the surface
- [ ] Any elements competing for attention in the frame (void is clean)
- [ ] Motion blur on the orb itself (smoothness is the point)
- [ ] Metallic finish (this is lacquer/obsidian, not chrome or gunmetal)

### Drift vs. The Other Orbs
Exo AI is the **anchor**. When other department orbs appear:
- Exo AI remains in its established position — it does not react, does not chase, does not move toward them
- The department orbs orbit around the space Exo AI defines
- Exo AI is larger or equal in scale to all others — never smaller
- If all orbs are present, Exo AI is front and center or the closest to camera
